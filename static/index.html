Power chart legend

Colors and markers follow battery power in watts (positive into the pack, negative out).

Charging
Segments where battery power is above zero: the system is drawing power into the battery from the adapter.
Discharging
Segments where battery power is below zero: the battery is feeding the system.
Mode switch
Dashed vertical lines mark times when power crossed zero between two samples, for example unplugging, plugging in, or switching between charging and supplying the load.

Temperature chart

The shaded bands use a 38 °C horizontal guide—the same threshold referenced in Charging Diagnosis when noting an elevated battery temperature.

Comfort
Readings below 38 °C are shown in the cooler-toned region. This is typical for light workloads and moderate charging.
Elevated
At or above 38 °C, the chart uses warmer colors. Elevated temperature is one reason macOS may reduce charge power.
Dashed vertical lines
When the trace crosses 38 °C between two samples, a vertical marker shows approximately when that crossing happened.

Battery chart legend

Colors use macOS charging flags from each stored sample. They describe power source and whether the pack is actively charging.

Charging
The battery is actively accepting charge while on AC or USB power.
On battery
Not on external power: the Mac is running from the internal battery.
External power
Plugged in, but not in an active charging state. Common when the battery is full or nearly full, when Optimized Battery Charging is holding the level, when charging is paused for temperature, or when the machine runs from the adapter while the pack stays idle.
Mode switch
Dashed vertical lines when the category changed between consecutive samples (charging, on battery, or external idle).
